# Introduction

Fusion Signage exposes many of the APIs we use internally to any customers who are interested in integrating with our platform. 

This guide will help you understand what our APIs are and how you can leverage them to enhance your digital signage workflows.

## What is an API?

An **API (Application Programming Interface)** is a set of rules and protocols that allows different software applications to communicate with each other. Think of it as a bridge that enables your own systems, apps, or scripts to "talk" to the Fusion Signage platform directly.

Instead of manually performing tasks through the Fusion Signage web interface, you can use our APIs to automate those same actions programmatically.

## Why use our APIs?

In the world of digital signage, automation and integration are key to scaling operations. APIs allow you to move beyond manual content management and create a more dynamic, data-driven signage network.

Here are some of the primary ways you can use the Fusion Signage APIs:

### Uploading and managing media
Automate your content pipeline by programmatically uploading images, videos, and other assets to your Fusion Signage account. 
- **Auto-sync:** Connect your internal creative DAM (Digital Asset Management) system directly to Fusion Signage.
- **Bulk operations:** Handle hundreds of assets at once without manual drag-and-drop.

### Creating and organising playlists
Dynamically manage what content plays and when.
- **Data-driven content:** Update playlists based on external triggers, such as weather, inventory levels, or local events.
- **Scheduling:** Programmatically create complex schedules and assign them to specific playlists.

### Managing screens and devices
Keep track of your entire fleet of screens from your own dashboard.
- <span class="badge badge--primary">Coming soon</span> **Status monitoring:** Integrate screen health data into your company's monitoring tools.
- <span class="badge badge--primary">Coming soon</span> **Remote commands:** Programmatically manage screen settings and assignments.
- <span class="badge badge--primary">Coming soon</span> **Scale:** Easily deploy configurations across thousands of screens simultaneously.
